A Little About Me

The environment where I grew up was complex, one that tested my strength from an early age. Despite the hardships around me, I always had my mother's support, as she worked hard to give me a place to grow and a foundation of love amid adversity. Although that pain once turned into frustration, I chose to channel that energy into a life philosophy rooted in empathy: I treat others with the kindness and support I would have liked to receive. That conscious decision to be a positive force is something I carry with great pride.

What drives me most is a boundless curiosity. I'm never satisfied with a surface-level understanding; I need to grasp the "how" and "why". This passion has led me to explore fascinating fields like space exploration (closely following milestones from NASA and SpaceX) and the world of technology and video games. I'm the kind of person who wakes up with a new question every morning, whether it's analyzing the economic miracle of Korea in the 80's or unraveling unsolved scientific mysteries.

In terms of how I learn, I'm visual and communicative. I pick things up quickly, especially when I can interact with the material and talk through it. And while I struggle with procrastination, I've learned to manage it through strategic planning, working with a clear structure that helps me ease the pressure and stay focused on doing things well.

My goal is clear: I want to contribute to the advancement of science and technology. I aspire to use my analytical mind and resilience to leave a real impact, proving that persistence and a hunger for knowledge can turn any adversity into an opportunity to innovate and help others.
